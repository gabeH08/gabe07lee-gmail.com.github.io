  • For all polynomials, first factor out the greatest common factor (also knwon as the GCF). We will show you a step by step on how to solve factor.

Here's an example of factoring:

STEP BY STEP ON HOW TO SOLVE FACTORING (Algebra ll)

Steps Example Explanation
Step 1: 42x^5-48x^4 First you observe the equation because you want to take out the greatest common factor out of it
Step 2: 6x(7x^5-8x^4) The GCF of this equation is 6x, therefore we should divide by 6x on both numbers 42x^5 and 48x^4
Step 3: 6x^4(7x-8) The GCF of this is ^4, so we divide ^4 from both numbers and take it outside of the parenthesis
Step 4: 6x^4(7x-8) Solution.
